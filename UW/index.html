WA Exposure Notification UW

Protect yourself, protect UW.

Starting on [[date]] UW rolled out use of WA Exposure Notification to (population at UW). WA Exposure Notification uses the Bluetooth on your smartphone to quickly notify you if you’ve had a possible exposure to COVID-19. This exposure notification technology is powered by Apple and Google, and allows you to:

  • Receive anonymous alerts if you have been in close contact with someone who tests positive for coronavirus.
  • Anonymously alert other users you were in close contact with if you test positive for coronavirus, without sharing any personal information.

WA Exposure Notification helps keep you and your fellow Huskies safe, and can help slow the spread of coronavirus (COVID-19) on the UW campus. It gives you an easy, automated way to find out if you’ve had a potential COVID-19 exposure, and connects you with the resources you need if you are exposed. It does all this while preserving your privacy, and ensuring you are in control of your information. Using WA Exposure Notification is voluntary and you can turn the system off or deactivate the app at any time. WA Exposure Notification will never collect, track, or store your location, GPS information, or personal information.

iPhone version

Download the pilot profile
Click to download the profile on Safari on your iPhone
Choose iPhone when it asks where to install

Install the pilot profile

Go to Settings > General > Profile (note that instead of “Profile” it might appear as “Profiles & Device Management”)
Tap on the profile “ENX Washington Pilot Configuration”
Tap “Install”
Enter your iPhone passcode (if passcode is enabled on your phone)
Tap “Install” again

Enable the Exposure Notifications

Go to Settings > Exposure Notifications > Turn on Exposure Notifications
Tap “Continue” and select United States > Washington
